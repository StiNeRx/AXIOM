Math as a Mother Tongue

A Proof of Concept for AXIOM Literacy

Target Demographic: Ages 4-7 (Pre-Primary)

TRADITIONAL APPROACH

"Here are 3 apples. Add 2. How many?"
Focus: Calculation

AXIOM LITERACY

"Here is a pile of apples. Are they inside the bowl or outside?"
Focus: Logic & Relationships

The Nouns

Set Theory & Logic

The Tool

"The Venn Hoops" — Two hula hoops on the floor.

The Lesson

Children sort a bucket of mixed toys into a "Red Things" hoop and a "Plastic Things" hoop, learning to categorize physically.

The "Reading" Moment

  • ∩ Learns this means "The Middle Part" (Intersection).
  • ∪ Learns this means "Everything" (Union).

The Adjectives

Topology & Space

The Tool

"The Clay Lab" — A simple ball of Play-Doh.

The Lesson

A child is shown how a coffee mug with a handle can be squished and morphed into a doughnut without tearing it.

The "Reading" Moment

  • ○ Understands a doughnut and a coffee mug are the "same word" because they both have one hole (Genus 1).

The Verbs

Vectors & Functions

The Tool

"The Arrow Floor" — A grid made of tape on the floor.

The Lesson

A child acts as a "particle," following cards with arrows (direction) and numbers (steps) to walk a path on the grid.

The "Reading" Moment

  • ↑ + → Physically performs vector addition, walking Up then Right to find their new position relative to the start (the hypotenuse).

The Expected Outcome: A Literacy Test

When shown two overlapping circles with a dot in the middle...

AXIOM Student Sees:

"Something that belongs to both groups."

(Logic)

When shown a coffee mug and a doughnut...

AXIOM Student Sees:

"Objects that both have one hole."

(Topology)
